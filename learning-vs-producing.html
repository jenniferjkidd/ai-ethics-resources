Decision Guide

Learning vs. Producing

Help preservice teachers decide when AI supports learning and when it bypasses the cognitive work students need to practice.

Start guideOpen stance template

Core Question

What are students learning, and what role should AI play in that learning?

Opening

A finished product is not always the learning goal.

AI can help people produce summaries, outlines, lesson ideas, explanations, feedback, images, code, emails, and polished writing. But in school, sometimes the goal is the thinking students do while producing the artifact.

Learning Task

The purpose is to develop knowledge, skill, judgment, fluency, confidence, or metacognition. AI risk: the tool may do the thinking students need to practice.

Production Task

The purpose is to create a useful product efficiently. AI may support drafting, editing, translation, formatting, brainstorming, or accessibility.

Demonstration Task

The purpose is to show what a student knows or can do. AI risk: the product may no longer be valid evidence of understanding.

Support Task

The purpose is to reduce unnecessary barriers while preserving the learning goal. AI may help with accessibility, organization, or feedback without replacing the target skill.

Decision Guide

Ask before assigning AI use

  1. What is the learning goal?
  2. What cognitive work matters most?
  3. What parts are practice?
  4. What parts are production or formatting?
  5. Where could AI support access without replacing learning?
  6. Where would AI make the evidence unreliable?
  7. What should students disclose?
  8. How will I assess process, judgment, or understanding?

Exit Ticket

Name one task where AI could support learning and one task where AI might undermine learning. Explain the difference.

Assignment Redesign

Sort the task

Students must do this themselves

Central thinking, practice, or demonstration of understanding.

AI may support this with disclosure

Helpful support that does not replace the learning goal.

AI may do this

Formatting or production work not central to learning.

AI should not be used here

Use would undermine evidence of learning.

Example redesign

Original: Write a one-page reflection about what you learned from your field observation.

Possible redesign: Use your field notes to identify one moment when a teacher supported student thinking. Discuss the moment with a partner. Then write a reflection explaining what you noticed, how you interpreted it, and what question it raises for your future teaching. You may use AI to proofread grammar after drafting, but not to generate the reflection. If you use AI, include a note explaining how.
