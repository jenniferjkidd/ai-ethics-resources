Case Study

Data Center Proposal

Jobs and innovation vs. water, energy, and community impact.

Start caseOpen worksheet

Best For

  • Foundations
  • Environmental justice
  • AI infrastructure
  • Community decision-making

Scenario

A community is offered a new AI data center.

A technology company proposes building a large data center near a growing town. The company promises jobs, tax revenue, school partnerships, and high-speed infrastructure. Local leaders are excited about economic development.

Community members ask about energy demand, water use, noise, land use, tax incentives, and whether local schools will benefit or bear costs. A nearby school district is also considering expanded AI tools that may rely on the same cloud infrastructure.

Decision Point

Should the community support the data center proposal? What conditions would make the decision more ethical?

Stakeholders

Who should be considered?

Students and Schools

May gain programs or infrastructure, but may also face indirect environmental and budget impacts.

Residents

May experience jobs, tax shifts, energy demands, water concerns, or land-use changes.

Local Government

Must weigh development claims against long-term public costs.

Technology Company

Benefits from infrastructure, incentives, and public approval.

Four-Question Analysis

Use the framework.

Who benefits?

The company, some workers, local officials, contractors, and possibly schools receiving partnerships or tax revenue.

Who risks harm?

Residents near the site, communities with stressed water or energy systems, and people excluded from decision-making.

Who pays the cost?

Communities may pay through resource demand, infrastructure strain, tax incentives, environmental effects, or opportunity costs.

Who gets to decide?

Public officials and corporate leaders may decide before students, families, environmental groups, and nearby residents have meaningful input.

Discussion Prompts

Questions for class discussion

  1. What information would the community need before deciding?
  2. What benefits should count, and who should receive them?
  3. How should schools discuss the hidden infrastructure behind AI tools?
  4. What conditions would make approval more responsible?

Possible Safeguards

  • Public environmental review
  • Transparent resource commitments
  • Community benefit agreement
  • School partnership accountability
  • Ongoing reporting
