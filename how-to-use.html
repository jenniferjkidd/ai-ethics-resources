How to Use This Hub

This hub is designed for teacher educator faculty who want to help preservice teachers think ethically about AI in education.

Start hereBrowse tools

Guiding Questions

What should preservice teachers know, notice, question, and be able to do? How can they develop ethical judgment, not just tool fluency?

Use a Converted Lesson

Converted lessons preserve the flow of student-facing AI literacy content: explanation, examples, reflection, and interactive elements.

Best for: class preparation, online modules, readings, and student-facing exploration.

Use a Teaching Plan

Teaching plans help faculty adapt a topic for a class session with learning goals, timing, discussion flow, facilitator notes, and extensions.

Best for: methods, foundations, ed tech, and field-experience seminars.

Use a Tool

Tools are worksheets, checklists, and templates that help students apply ethical judgment to specific AI decisions.

Best for: small-group work, assignment add-ons, field-placement reflection, and course projects.

Use a Case

Cases are short dilemmas that invite discussion and judgment without a single easy answer.

Best for: opening discussions, exit tickets, role-play, and framework application.
