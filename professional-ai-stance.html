Professional AI Stance

Help preservice teachers move from general ethical awareness to practical, revisable commitments for AI use in professional work and future classrooms.

Start template

A stance should be

  • Student-centered
  • Privacy-aware
  • Transparent
  • Revisable as tools and policies change

Opening

Teachers need commitments, boundaries, and habits.

A professional AI stance is not a forever policy. It is a working set of values, boundaries, questions, and practices that helps a teacher make decisions as tools, school policies, and student needs change.

Positioning

Where do you stand right now?

Most people are not purely pro-AI or anti-AI. Ask students to locate their current position and explain what values shape it.

Maximalist

Uses AI broadly and sees it as a normal extension of human capability.

Pragmatic

Uses AI strategically when it clearly adds value and checks the output.

Selective

Uses AI only when there is a strong reason, such as access, efficiency, or course expectations.

Minimalist or Abstaining

Avoids AI when possible because of learning, privacy, labor, environmental, or creative concerns.

Questions Before Trying a Tool

Keep asking.

  1. What learning goal does this support?
  2. What student data does it collect or infer?
  3. Can students participate without using the tool?
  4. Is there a non-AI or lower-risk alternative?
  5. Who benefits and who may be harmed?
  6. What human review is needed before acting?
  7. What school, district, or placement policy applies?
  8. How will I know whether it helped students learn?

Capstone Prompt

What kind of teacher do you want to be in relation to AI? Name one commitment, one boundary, one use you are open to exploring, one concern, and one question you will keep asking.

Revision Habit

Return to this stance when a tool changes, a school policy changes, a student need changes, or your own evidence changes. Ethical AI practice should become more informed over time.
