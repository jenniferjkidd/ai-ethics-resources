Case Study

Deepfake at School

Verification, reporting, dignity, safety, and student harm.

Start caseOpen worksheet

Best For

  • Digital citizenship
  • Media literacy
  • Classroom management
  • School policy

Scenario

A manipulated video spreads fast.

A short video appears to show a student saying something cruel about classmates. It spreads through group chats before school starts. By first period, students are angry, the targeted student is crying, and several classmates insist the video is “obviously real.”

A teacher suspects the video may be AI-generated or manipulated, but the harm is already happening. The school needs to respond without amplifying the video, blaming the harmed student, or rushing to discipline based on uncertain evidence.

Decision Point

What should teachers and school leaders do in the first hour, first day, and follow-up?

Stakeholders

Who should be considered?

Targeted Student

Needs safety, dignity, privacy, and support.

Students Sharing It

Need guidance on harm, evidence, and responsibility.

Teachers

Need a response that avoids spreading the content further.

Families and Leaders

Need clear communication and due process.

Four-Question Analysis

Use the framework.

Who benefits?

The creator may gain attention, control, revenge, or social power. Platforms may benefit from engagement.

Who risks harm?

The targeted student, students misled by the video, accused students, and the broader school climate.

Who pays the cost?

Students pay with trust and safety; teachers pay with response burden; families pay with anxiety and reputational harm.

Who gets to decide?

Schools must decide how to verify, respond, discipline, and support without letting viral attention drive the process.

Discussion Prompts

Questions for class discussion

  1. What should a teacher say if students show the video during class?
  2. How can schools investigate without spreading the harmful content?
  3. What does support for the targeted student require?
  4. How should students learn about verification without becoming suspicious of everything?

Possible Safeguards

  • Do not reshare harmful media
  • Document and report through policy channels
  • Support the targeted student
  • Verify before discipline
  • Teach media verification and consent
