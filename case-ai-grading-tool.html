Case Study

AI Grading Tool

Efficiency vs. fairness, validity, transparency, and teacher judgment.

Start caseOpen worksheet

Best For

  • Assessment
  • Methods courses
  • Educational technology
  • Teacher professionalism

Scenario

A school wants faster feedback.

A department is considering an AI grading assistant for student writing. The tool scores drafts against a rubric, highlights strengths and weaknesses, and suggests feedback comments teachers can accept or edit.

Teachers are overwhelmed by grading loads and want students to receive feedback quickly. But students worry that the tool may misunderstand voice, language background, disability-related writing differences, creative approaches, or culturally specific examples.

Decision Point

Should teachers use the AI grading tool for student writing? What should remain under human judgment?

Stakeholders

Who should be considered?

Students

Need fair, valid, explainable feedback that supports growth.

Teachers

Need workload support without surrendering professional judgment.

Families

May question whether grades reflect human evaluation.

School Leaders

Must ensure consistency, equity, appeals, and policy alignment.

Four-Question Analysis

Use the framework.

Who benefits?

Teachers may save time; students may receive faster formative feedback; schools may standardize scoring.

Who risks harm?

Students whose writing does not match the tool’s learned patterns, multilingual learners, students with disabilities, and students appealing unclear scores.

Who pays the cost?

Students may pay with reduced trust or misjudgment; teachers may pay with deskilling or pressure to accept automated scores.

Who gets to decide?

Teachers, students, and families may have little say if adoption is mandated at a department or district level.

Discussion Prompts

Questions for class discussion

  1. What kinds of feedback could AI reasonably support?
  2. What kinds of assessment should remain human-led?
  3. Should students be told when AI helped generate feedback or scores?
  4. What appeal process should exist if a student disagrees?

Possible Safeguards

  • Teacher review before scores count
  • Student disclosure
  • Appeal process
  • Bias and validity checks
  • Use for formative feedback only
