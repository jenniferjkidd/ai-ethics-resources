AI Tool Safety Checklist

Use this before asking students to rely on an AI tool for feedback, tutoring, writing support, assessment, images, companionship, planning, or decision-making.

Start checklistOpen ethics worksheet

Look For

  • Age appropriateness
  • Human oversight
  • Privacy protections
  • Clear escalation plans

1. Purpose and Fit

  • Is the learning purpose clear?
  • Could a lower-risk tool or non-AI activity meet the same goal?
  • Does the tool support learning rather than replace needed practice?

2. Age and Context

  • Is the tool appropriate for the age group?
  • Are terms of service compatible with student use?
  • Would families reasonably expect this kind of tool in school?

3. Privacy and Data

  • What information is collected?
  • Can prompts, uploads, images, or student work be retained or used for training?
  • Can students participate without entering sensitive information?

4. Accuracy and Bias

  • How will errors be detected?
  • Could outputs disadvantage students by language, disability, race, culture, or access?
  • Can students challenge or question the tool?

5. Human Oversight

  • Who reviews outputs before they affect students?
  • Who is accountable when the tool is wrong?
  • Are high-stakes decisions kept with humans?

6. Wellbeing and Escalation

  • Could students disclose sensitive personal information?
  • What happens if a student expresses distress or risk?
  • Are students directed to appropriate human support?

7. Transparency and Consent

  • Will students know when AI is being used?
  • Do students or families need notification, consent, or an alternative?
  • Can use be explained in plain language?

8. Decision

  • Use as planned
  • Use with safeguards
  • Teacher-use only
  • Seek approval
  • Do not use with students
