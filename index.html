Tools and Evaluation Guides

Build these first

These tools make the hub immediately useful, even before every full module is polished.

WorksheetFirst Release

AI Ethics Review Worksheet

Apply the four-question framework to classroom tools, school systems, or institutional AI decisions.

  • Who benefits?
  • Who risks harm?
  • Who pays the cost?
  • Who gets to decide?
ChecklistFirst Release

AI Tool Privacy Audit

Help teachers decide what should never be uploaded and what policy questions must be asked before tool use.

  • What data is collected?
  • Can prompts or uploads train future models?
  • Who can access student information?
  • Can students opt out?
Decision GuideFirst Release

Learning vs. Producing

Clarify whether AI is supporting the learning goal or bypassing the cognitive work students need.

  • What must students learn to do?
  • What can be safely offloaded?
  • What evidence will show understanding?
TemplateFirst Release

Professional AI Stance Template

Support preservice teachers in drafting boundaries for their own work and their future classroom practice.

  • AI I may use for my work
  • AI I may use with students
  • AI I will not use with students
  • Uses requiring approval
MapNext Build

School AI Policy Map

Trace where AI rules come from: classroom norms, school policy, district procurement, state guidance, federal law, and platform terms.

AI Tool Safety Checklist

Evaluate guardrails, age appropriateness, crisis escalation, hallucination handling, bias testing, and human oversight.

Faculty PD Flow

A session arc for Team AI ethics work

Use the hub to help faculty select resources for their own courses and name what preservice teachers need to practice.

  1. Open: What should preservice teachers know, notice, question, and be able to do?
  2. Frame: Apply the four-question ethics framework to one AI education tool.
  3. Focus: Explore privacy, bias, learning, wellbeing, or governance.
  4. Apply: Choose one course assignment or topic to adapt.
  5. Commit: Draft one AI-use principle for preservice teachers.
