# AI Ethics Resource Hub Architecture

Working purpose: organize AI ethics resources for teacher educator faculty who are preparing preservice teachers to understand, evaluate, and responsibly integrate AI in educational settings.

Current working focus: Ethics of AI. The broader hub can eventually connect to all five UNESCO dimensions, but the immediate curation work should prioritize the Ethics of AI strand and its connections to classroom decision-making, educational use cases, and preservice teacher preparation.

This plan uses UNESCO's AI Competency Framework for Teachers as a guide, but not as the only structure. The public-facing hub should help faculty quickly find teachable resources, while also helping preservice teachers build ethical judgment for classroom use.

## Primary Audiences

### Teacher Educator Faculty

Faculty need resources they can integrate into existing teacher-preparation courses without redesigning the whole course around AI. They need:

- short, ready-to-teach modules;
- discussion prompts and cases;
- classroom activities;
- student-facing explainers;
- links to interactive tools;
- guidance for adapting materials by grade band, subject area, and course context.

### Preservice Teachers

Preservice teachers need to understand AI broadly, but also in relation to their future professional responsibilities. They should be able to:

- explain what AI is and how common AI tools work at a practical level;
- identify ethical risks in educational uses of AI;
- evaluate whether an AI tool is appropriate for a learning goal, learner population, and local context;
- protect student privacy, agency, inclusion, and well-being;
- recognize bias, misinformation, hallucination, and unequal access;
- design AI-supported learning activities that preserve human judgment and teacher-student relationships;
- teach students to use AI critically, transparently, and responsibly.

## Organizing Spine

Use the UNESCO framework as a crosswalk, not as the navigation by itself. For the current phase, use the Ethics of AI dimension as the primary filter while noting meaningful connections to the other dimensions.

UNESCO dimensions:

1. Human-centred mindset
2. Ethics of AI
3. AI foundations and applications
4. AI pedagogy
5. AI for professional learning

UNESCO progression levels:

1. Acquire: basic AI literacy and initial ethical use
2. Deepen: responsible integration into teaching and learning
3. Create: leadership, adaptation, policy, and innovation

For this hub, resources should be tagged by both dimension and level, but the site navigation should use teacher-friendly categories.

Current curation priority:

1. Ethics of AI as the primary dimension.
2. Human-centred mindset as a close companion, because ethical AI use in education depends on human agency, accountability, and learner well-being.
3. AI pedagogy as the applied context, because preservice teachers need to make ethical decisions inside lesson design, assessment, feedback, and student AI use.
4. AI foundations and applications only as needed to help learners understand ethical risks.
5. AI for professional learning only when faculty or preservice teachers are considering their own responsible AI use.

## Proposed Competency Map for Preservice Teachers

### 1. Explain AI in Human Terms

Preservice teachers can explain that AI systems are human-designed, data-driven tools whose outputs reflect design choices, training data, business models, and use contexts.

UNESCO alignment: Human-centred mindset; AI foundations; Acquire.

### 2. Protect Student Rights and Well-being

Preservice teachers can identify privacy, safety, age-appropriateness, accessibility, and student agency concerns before using AI tools with learners.

UNESCO alignment: Ethics of AI; Human-centred mindset; Acquire and Deepen.

### 3. Evaluate Bias, Representation, and Harm

Preservice teachers can examine AI outputs and tool designs for bias, stereotypes, linguistic/cultural exclusion, disability access concerns, and uneven impacts on marginalized learners.

UNESCO alignment: Ethics of AI; AI foundations; Deepen.

### 4. Use AI for Pedagogical Purposes, Not Convenience Alone

Preservice teachers can decide when AI supports a learning goal and when it weakens learning, relationships, creativity, assessment validity, or student thinking.

UNESCO alignment: AI pedagogy; Human-centred mindset; Deepen.

### 5. Design Transparent and Responsible Student AI Use

Preservice teachers can design activities where students use AI with clear purpose, disclosure expectations, process reflection, and human judgment.

UNESCO alignment: AI pedagogy; Ethics of AI; Deepen.

### 6. Critique AI Systems and Policies

Preservice teachers can participate in conversations about classroom, school, or district AI guidelines, including who benefits, who is at risk, and what safeguards are needed.

UNESCO alignment: Ethics of AI; AI for professional learning; Create.

### 7. Continue Learning as AI Changes

Preservice teachers can use professional learning habits to keep evaluating new tools, evidence, policies, and ethical concerns over time.

UNESCO alignment: AI for professional learning; Acquire, Deepen, Create.
