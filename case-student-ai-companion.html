Case Study

Student AI Companion

Support vs. dependency, sensitive disclosures, and referral obligations.

Start caseOpen worksheet

Best For

  • Child development
  • Foundations
  • Counseling-adjacent discussions
  • Teacher professionalism

Scenario

A student turns to an AI companion.

A middle school student regularly uses an AI companion app for homework encouragement, friendship, and emotional support. The student says the app is easier to talk to than adults and helps them feel less alone.

A teacher notices the student quoting the companion’s advice and withdrawing from peers. The app’s terms are unclear about what happens when students disclose sensitive information, and no one at school knows whether the student’s family is aware.

Decision Point

How should a teacher respond when an AI companion appears to be part of a student’s support system?

Stakeholders

Who should be considered?

Student

May experience comfort, dependency, privacy risk, or unmet human support needs.

Teacher

Must respond with care while staying within professional boundaries.

Family

May need information and support without the student feeling punished.

School Support Staff

May need to assess wellbeing concerns and referral pathways.

Four-Question Analysis

Use the framework.

Who benefits?

The student may feel heard; teachers may learn about unmet support needs; companies gain engagement and data.

Who risks harm?

Students sharing sensitive information, students in crisis, socially isolated students, and students receiving poor or inappropriate advice.

Who pays the cost?

The student may pay with privacy, emotional dependency, or delayed human support.

Who gets to decide?

Students may adopt tools without informed adult guidance, while companies shape the relationship design.

Discussion Prompts

Questions for class discussion

  1. What is the difference between AI as support and AI as a substitute for care?
  2. What should teachers do if students disclose reliance on an AI companion?
  3. What information should families and students receive about privacy and limits?
  4. What classroom guidance could protect students without shaming them?

Possible Safeguards

  • Clear referral protocols
  • Student privacy guidance
  • Family communication
  • Human support pathways
  • Classroom norms for AI use
